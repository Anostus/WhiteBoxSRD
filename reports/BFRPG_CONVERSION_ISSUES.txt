BFRPG to White Box monster conversion notes
Generated for the White Box SRD site update. Updated to split multi-monster entries into individual pages.

Converted entries added: 189.
The source used was Basic Fantasy Role-Playing Game, 4th Edition Release 142, © 2006–2025 Chris Gonnerman, CC BY-SA 4.0. Converted creature pages contain the same source note.

General conversion decisions:
- Ascending AC was converted as BFRPG AC - 1, then shown in the White Box descending/ascending format (for example, BFRPG AC 14 becomes 6 [13]).
- Hit Dice were preserved, but asterisks were treated as additional HDE steps. For fractional-HD creatures with an asterisk, I rounded the resulting fractional HDE up to the next whole HDE.
- Multiple-attack routines were compressed to one White Box-style attack, generally 1d6 damage, adjusted upward or downward for very large, very small, or especially dangerous creatures.
- BFRPG entries that presented separate variants in one table have now been split into individual White Box pages and individual index rows. Index/table names use a dagger (†) rather than “(BFRPG)”; the footnote explains that those entries are converted creatures.
- Existing White Box monsters with obvious close equivalents were not duplicated unless the BFRPG entry contained a missing variant or a major power difference. Examples imported despite overlap include Basilisk variants, Blink Dog/Flicker Beast, Ghast, normal rats, Trollwife, and Alicorn.

Specific conversion issues and judgment calls:
- Color dragons already present in White Box were not duplicated. BFRPG-only dragon types such as Cloud, Plains/Yellow, Sea/Gray, and Dragon Turtle were converted. Dragon age categories and breath details are condensed; the original BFRPG source statistics remain in the details element on each page.
- Elementals already present in White Box (air, earth, fire, water) were skipped. BFRPG-only elemental types (cold, lightning, metal, wood) were added and their staff/device/spell variants were split into separate pages.
- Golems already present in White Box were skipped unless BFRPG supplied a clearly missing type (amber, bone, bronze, clay). Bonus attack values or parenthetical attack bonuses in BFRPG stat lines were not counted as Hit Dice.
- Several animal and prehistoric animal entries have multiple listed attacks in BFRPG; these were compressed heavily, so damage is intentionally more White Box-like than Basic Fantasy-like.
- Plant, vermin, swarm, disease, and parasite monsters such as rot grubs, molds, insect swarms, blood roses, strangle vines, and tentacle worms require referee judgment because their threat is mostly special effects rather than damage. Their HDE may understate or overstate danger depending on party resources.
- Variable-range entries such as Blood Rose, Insect Swarm, Trollkin, and Giant Water Termite were split into representative HD-specific pages to remove semicolon-separated statistics from the indexes.
- Creatures skipped as obvious equivalents or alias references include many core humanoids, undead, dragons, and classic monsters already present in the White Box SRD. See the list below.
