Golem, Bronze

Armor Class
0 [19] (m)
Hit Dice
20
Attacks
Fist (1d6+2 plus heat)
Special
magic weapons to hit, golem immunities/berserk, heat damage, molten spray when hit
Move
24
HDE/XP
22/5,000

Description

These golems resemble statues made of bronze; unlike natural bronze statues, they never turn green from verdigris. A bronze golem is 10 feet tall and weighs about 4,500 pounds. A bronze golem cannot speak or make any vocal noise, nor does it have any distinguishable odor. It moves with a ponderous but smooth gait. Each step causes the floor to tremble unless it is on a thick, solid foundation. The interior of a bronze golem is molten metal. Creatures hit by one in combat suffer an additional 1d10 points of damage from the heat (unless resistant to heat or fire). If one is hit in combat, molten metal spurts out, spraying the attacker for 2d6 damage. A save vs. Death Ray is allowed to avoid the metal spray.

Golem Notes

Golems are magically animated constructs most often built as guardians or laborers, but these golem entries are no longer all man-shaped and they do not all share one universal rules package. Most require magical weapons to hit or have special weapon restrictions, while a few specify silver, a minimum weapon bonus, reduced damage from certain weapon types, or vulnerability to fire.

Some converted Basic Fantasy golems explicitly carry golem immunities and berserk rules, while many White Box and Field Guide variants instead define their own spell interactions: lightning may heal one golem, fire may slow or heal another, rock to mud can damage or heal stone, and other golems cast spells, become invisible, breathe poison or fire, grapple, constrict, suffocate, inflict poison, reflect damage, or use construction-specific hazards.

Use the individual golem entries as the source of truth for weapon requirements, spell immunities, berserk behavior, healing or slowing interactions, grapples, breath weapons, and material-specific vulnerabilities.
