Smouldergeist

12

Attack (12d6)

Description

Smouldergeists appear as humanoid-shaped accumulations of smog and heat. They are soot- colored, save for white-hot circles where their eyes might be. They are pyromaniacs and spawn around sources of extreme heat. They enjoy blasting fire bolts at anything and everything, the sound of which prevents them from gaining surprise in an encounter. Smouldergeists are restless souls; they cannot be put at ease. They just want everything to burn.

A smouldergeist typically flings bolts of fire at its opponents but roughly once per hour may send out an intense burst of flame up to a 30-foot radius. A save vs. Dragon Breath allows a victim to take half the damage rolled. Additionally, the smouldergeist may de- materialize into a cloud of smog, also filling a space up to a 30-foot radius. Those caught in the cloud must save vs. Poison or begin taking 1d8 points of damage for each round spent in it choking. A smouldergeist may move through closed portals, but this takes a full round and is obvious to any observers on the other side of the portal.
