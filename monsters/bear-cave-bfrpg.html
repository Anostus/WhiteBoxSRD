Bear, Cave

Armor Class
5 [14]
Hit Dice
7
Attacks
Claw or bite (1d6+1)
Special
hug 2d8, fights 1 round at 0 HP
Move
12
HDE/XP
7/600

Description

These monstrous bears are even larger than brown bears, with one weighing up to 1,800 pounds and when on all four feet are up to six feet high at the shoulder. They are ferocious killers, attacking almost anything of equal or smaller size.

On a natural 19-20 with a claw attack, a cave bear catches the victim in a hug for 2d8 extra damage. All bears are very tough to kill, and are able to move and attack for one round after losing all hit points.

Bear Notes

Bears are strong claw-and-bite combatants, but their exact hug rule now varies by entry. The Basic Fantasy-derived black, cave, grizzly, and polar bears trigger their hug on a natural 19–20 with a claw attack and can still move and attack for one round after being reduced to 0 hit points. Many Field Guide variants instead use the two-claw hug procedure or list only a generic hug special; use the individual monster page for the damage and trigger.

The converted bear family now includes several exceptions: battle bears fight with claws and shoulder spears but do not bite effectively, dream bears inflict the “bear in mind” curse, ghost bears require magical weapons and add fear, Constitution drain, turning, and undead immunities, long bears can chain additional claw pairs against large enough victims, migou use phantasmal paralysis, quill-bears deal quill damage and regenerate in moonlight, and the ursine behemoth can only hug suitably large opponents.

The individual entries are the source of truth for exact hug damage, immunities, spell-like effects, regeneration, and other special rules.
