Eel, Giant

Armor Class
8 [11]
Hit Dice
2, 4, or 6
Attacks
Bite (1d6/1d6+1/1d6+2)
Special
1-in-6 can shock 3/day (1d4 per HD, 20-ft. radius, save half), electricity resistance
Move
Swim 18
HDE/XP
2/30, 4/120, or 6/400 (electric: 3/60, 5/240, or 7/600)

Description

A Giant Eel is the huge cousin to the common eel, and is similar in most ways except size. This great aquatic beast is often found in lost underwater ruins or as a guardian raised and trained by underwater races.

One in six giant eels can emit an electrical shock up to three times per day, affecting those within a 20-foot radius. The shock causes 1d4 points of damage for each hit die of the giant eel; a save vs. Dragon Breath is allowed for half damage. During a round when a giant electric eel uses its shock, it is immune to electrical attacks. On other rounds it has a +2 bonus on saves against electrical attacks.

Eel Notes

This subcategory currently contains only the giant eel and weed eel entries; older common-eel and separate electric-eel text has been removed from the category description because those creatures are not members here.

Giant eels are large aquatic predators with 2, 4, or 6 Hit Dice. One in six giant eels can emit an electrical shock up to three times per day in a 20-foot radius, dealing 1d4 damage per Hit Die with a save vs. Dragon Breath for half damage. A giant electric eel is immune to electrical attacks during the round it shocks and has a +2 bonus on saves against electrical attacks at other times.

Weed eels are venomous ambush predators whose camouflage lets them surprise on 1–5 on 1d6. Their bite kills a living victim that fails a save vs. Poison, and colonies use clustered tunnel entrances to look like patches of seaweed.
